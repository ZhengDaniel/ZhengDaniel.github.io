Tangzhong

  • 5 Tbsp water
  • 5 Tbsp whole milk
  • 3 Tbsp bread flour

Bread Dough

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1/3 cup dry milk powder
  • 1 tbsp yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs (room temp)
  • 3/4 cup whole milk (room temp)
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter (melted)
  • 2 tbsp milk

Directions

  • Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  • To make the Tangzhong, mix Tangzhong ingredients until no lumps
  • In a saucepan, continually stir until it becomes a thick, pudding like consistency
  • Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool
  • In a large bowl, whisk together dry ingredients
  • In another bowl, mix wet ingredients
  • Gradually, pour wet ingredients with dry ingredients while mixing
  • Add more flour little by little if the dough is too dry
  • If using a stand mixer or bread machine, let dough knead on medium for ~10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic
  • If kneading by hand, knead for ~10-20 minutes until smooth and elastic
  • Perform the window pane test* to check if the dough is well developed
  • Shape into a ball and place in lightly greased bowl
  • Cover bowl and place in a warm place until doubled for ~1-1.5 hours
  • After doubled, poke middle with a finger. If dough does not spring back, it is ready
  • Dump the dough on a floured surface and shape dough into balls.
  • Space them on a lined sheet and place in a warm place to rise until doubled. ~45 min.
  • Brush the surface of dough balls gently with the 2 tbsps of milk. Make sure to do it gently because you don't want to deflate the dough
  • Bake for 20-22 min until golden brown.
  • Let cool for 5 min. Serve immediately.
