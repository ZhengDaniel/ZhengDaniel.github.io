Cinnamon Rolls

by Olivia Kuang

Tangzhong Method:

  • 5 Tbsp water
  • 5 Tbsp whole milk
  • 3 Tbsp bread flour

Bread

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1/3 cup dry milk powder
  • 1 tbsp yeast
  • 1 3/4 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs (room temp)
  • 3/4 cup whole milk (room temp)
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter (melted)

Filling:

  • 2/3 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp cinnamon

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. To make the Tangzhong, mix Tangzhong ingredients until no lumps
  3. In a saucepan, continually stir until it becomes a thick, pudding like consistency
  4. Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool
  5. In a large bowl, whisk together dry ingredients
  6. In another bowl, mix wet ingredients
  7. Gradually, pour wet ingredients with dry ingredients while mixing
  8. Add more flour little by little if the dough is too dry
  9. If using a stand mixer or bread machine, let dough knead on medium for ~10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic
  10. If kneading by hand, knead for ~10-20 minutes until smooth and elastic
  11. Perform the window pane test* to check if the dough is well developed
  12. Shape into a ball and place in lightly greased bowl
  13. Cover bowl and place in a warm place until doubled for ~1-1.5 hours
  14. After doubled, poke middle with a finger. If dough does not spring back, it is ready
  15. Dump the dough on a floured surface and using either a rolling pin or your fingers, form a rectangle
  16. Sprinkle filling generously over rectangle
  17. From the longer side, roll dough tightly to form a log
  18. Using a sharp knife, cut pieces into your desired length (If you want a doughier roll or more filling)
  19. Place into a pan, spaced about 1 inch apart
  20. Cover and let rise for ~45 minutes
  21. Bake for 20-23 minutes. It should be nicely browned
  22. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving. Accompany with glaze if desired
