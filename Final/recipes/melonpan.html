Melon Pan

Ingredients

  • 5 Tbsp water
  • 5 Tbsp whole milk
  • 3 Tbsp bread flour
  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1/3 cup dry milk powder
  • 1 tbsp yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs (room temp)
  • 3/4 cup whole milk (room temp)
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter (melted)

Biscuit Crust

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter(softened)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg (beaten)
  • 1 1/2 cup + 2 tbsp cake flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder

Directions

    • Preheat oven to 350 degrees
    • To make the Tangzhong, mix Tangzhong ingredients until no lumps
    • In a saucepan, continually stir until it becomes a thick, pudding like consistency
    • Transfer to a bowl and set aside to cool
    • In a large bowl, whisk together dry ingredients
    • In another bowl, mix wet ingredients
    • Gradually, pour wet ingredients with dry ingredients while mixing
    • Add more flour little by little if the dough is too dry
    • If using a stand mixer or bread machine, let dough knead on medium for ~10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic
    • If kneading by hand, knead for ~10-20 minutes until smooth and elastic
    • Perform the window pane test* to check if the dough is well developed
    • Shape into a ball and place in lightly greased bowl
    • Cover bowl and place in a warm place until doubled for ~1-1.5 hours
    • To make the biscuit crust, beat softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
    • Gradually add in beaten egg. Make sure to not overmix.
    • Sift in baking powder and cake flour. Mix to combine.
    • Dump out dough onto a lightly floured surface and form a log.
    • Cut log into 10 pieces and roll them into balls. Refigerate for 10 min until hardened a bit.
    • After dough is doubled, poke middle with a finger. If dough does not spring back, it is ready
    • Dump the dough on a floured surface and shape dough into 10 balls. Let rest for 15 min.
    • Take out biscuit dough and using a rolling pin or your fingers, flatten into a circle.
    • Flatten bread dough and fold into thirds, then thirds again. Gather all the sides together to form a ball.
    • Take each ball and place it in the middle of the biscuit dough seam side up.
    • Fold the sides of the biscuit dough up to wrap around the bread dough.
    • Flip and place on lined sheet. Cover and let rise for ~45 min
    • Bake for 20 min.
    • Let cool for 5 min and serve immediately.
